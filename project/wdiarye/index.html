week 7

I am thinking of creating a game that is reminiscent of a physical childhood game. The childhood game involves two teams of humans. It starts with the two teams (let's call them A and B) lined up on either end (end j and end k) of the same side of the square (side x). The goal (and the way to win the game) is to have the entire team reach the other end of side x by running along the other 3 sides of the square (the path). In other words, A's goal is to reach end j from end k; B's goal to reach end k from end j. However, there can only be one human from each team running along the path at any point in time. And, when the humans from each side of the team meets along the path, they'd have to engage in a battle of scissors, paper, stone. Let's say the human from team A looses. This first human on team A (A1, for short) would then have to go back to end k, at the back of the human line. At the same time, A2 may start their attempt at reaching end j as B1 continues their approach towards end k. When A2 and B1 meet along the path, they would then have to engage in the battle of scissors, paper, stone again. Let's say B1 reaches end k before anyone from team A reaches end j. B2 may then start their attempt at reaching end k.


The game I'm thinking of creating would be similar, but with a few changes:

  • End A and end B would be along two opposite sides of the square, and on opposing ends.
  • The path would be within the square, which would be transformed into a maze.
  • The humans would be able to avoid one another in the maze since there would be multiple paths each human can take. In a similar vein, each player may also strategise and attempt to block their opposing team from reaching their end by meeting them in the path and enngaging in a battle of scissors, paper, stone with them. (Losing the battle in this online game would similarly entail the loser returning to their starting point--they'd be teleported back.)


This game might be quite a lot, so I'm thinking of just starting with a single player game that just requires the player to solve the maze by going through it. I would then build on it, depending on how much time I've got.
